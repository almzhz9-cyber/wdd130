Jalisco, Mexico

Mexico is a vast and diverse country of over 130 million people, rich in ancient civilizations like the Aztec and Maya, stunning landscapes ranging from deserts to jungles to coastlines, and a cultural vibrancy felt in every city and village. At the heart of its world-famous cuisine are tacos — small corn tortillas filled with everything from al pastor (spit-roasted marinated pork with pineapple) to carnitas (slow-braised crispy pork), barbacoa (overnight slow-cooked beef or lamb), and birria (a rich stewed meat from Jalisco served with dipping consommé) — always finished simply with raw onion, cilantro, fresh salsa, and a squeeze of lime, reflecting a street food culture that is deeply woven into daily Mexican life.
